About Mviringo Africa

Mviringo Africa emerged from the discussions held during the Planeteer Wind Summit on July 29th, 2023, where the concept of a circular economy was the main point of discussion. Natalie Bullut inspired by the desire to address plastic pollution at Catholic University collaborated with Ruth Tanui to design the first project. The first projectâ€™s aim was to curb plastic pollution at the Catholic University of Eastern Africa by strategically placing segregated bins to promote recycling of plastics as one of its solutions. With a focus on circular economy, Mviringo further works to promote climate action through education, meaningful youth involvement, gender equality in sustainable development, and responsible production and consumption.

Our Mission and Vision

Our Mission

Our mission is to transform communities through sustainable solutions in light of the various environmental and climate-related problems they face. We aim to empower individuals and communities to take action in addressing climate challenges by promoting sustainable practices, fostering community resilience, and advocating for environmental justice.

Our Vision

A world where communities thrive in harmony with nature, empowered by sustainable practices and a collective commitment to preserving the planet for future generations. Mviringo Africa envisions a future where environmental sustainability is woven into the fabric of everyday life, ensuring a healthy planet for all.

Our Key Initiatives

  • fostering a sustainable future for Africa by involving communities in environmental stewardship, education, and innovation.
  • Advocating for and implementing circular economy solutions to rethink consumption habits.
  • Uniting communities and businesses to combat plastic pollution and its environmental consequences.
  • Empowering individuals through education and innovation to become environmental change-makers.

Promoting youth inclusion in finding and implementing sustainable solutions to tackle environmental and climate relate problems.
